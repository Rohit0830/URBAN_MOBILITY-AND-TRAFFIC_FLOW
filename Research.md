# Urban Mobility & Traffic Flow  
## End-to-End Data Analysis Research

---

## 1. Introduction

Urban mobility plays a critical role in the economic efficiency, safety, and quality of life in modern cities. Rapid urbanization and increased vehicle ownership have resulted in traffic congestion, longer travel times, higher accident rates, and environmental concerns.

This research focuses on analyzing **urban mobility and traffic flow data** to identify congestion patterns, safety risks, and inefficiencies in transportation systems. The objective is to provide **data-driven insights** that can support better traffic management, infrastructure planning, and mobility policy decisions.

---

## 2. Objectives

The key objectives of this analysis are:

- To identify **traffic congestion patterns** across time and locations  
- To analyze **traffic flow efficiency** using volume and speed metrics  
- To understand **urban mobility trends** across different transport modes  
- To detect **high-risk areas and time periods** affecting road safety  
- To provide **actionable recommendations** for improving urban mobility  

---

## 3. Business Problem Statement

Urban transportation authorities face challenges such as:

- Peak-hour congestion causing travel delays  
- Uneven utilization of road infrastructure  
- High accident rates at specific locations  
- Inefficient traffic flow at intersections  
- Limited data-driven decision-making  

This project aims to answer:
> *How can urban traffic and mobility data be used to improve traffic flow, safety, and overall transportation efficiency?*

---

## 4. Dataset Overview

The analysis uses structured urban mobility and traffic datasets containing information such as:

- Date and time of observation  
- Location (road, junction, or area)  
- Traffic volume  
- Average vehicle speed  
- Delay or congestion indicators  
- Transport mode / vehicle type  
- Weather and road conditions (where available)  

### Data Sources
- Open government transport datasets  
- City traffic and mobility open data portals  
- Public datasets from platforms such as Kaggle  

---

## 5. Tools & Technologies

The project is developed using the following tools:

- **Python**  
  - Data cleaning and preprocessing  
  - Exploratory data analysis (EDA)  
  - Feature creation and aggregation  

- **Excel**  
  - Data validation and manual cross-checking  
  - Pivot-based summaries  

- **Power BI**  
  - Interactive dashboards  
  - KPI tracking and visualization  
  - Insight-driven storytelling  

> Note: The analysis avoids machine learning and complex DAX to keep results interpretable and business-focused.

---

## 6. Methodology

### 6.1 Data Cleaning
- Removal of duplicate records  
- Handling missing and inconsistent values  
- Standardization of categorical variables  
- Conversion of date and time fields  
- Creation of derived columns such as:
  - Hour of day  
  - Day of week  
  - Peak / Off-peak classification  

### 6.2 Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)
- Traffic volume trends across time  
- Speed vs traffic volume relationships  
- Location-wise traffic distribution  
- Mode-wise and vehicle-wise movement patterns  

### 6.3 Metric Creation
Key metrics derived include:
- Average traffic volume per hour  
- Average speed by location  
- Congestion index  
- Peak-hour traffic share  
- Accident or delay concentration (where applicable)  

---

## 7. Key Analysis Areas

### 7.1 Traffic Congestion Analysis
- Identification of peak congestion hours  
- Roads and areas with consistently high traffic load  
- Comparison of peak vs off-peak traffic behavior  

### 7.2 Urban Mobility Patterns
- Usage trends across different transport modes  
- Temporal demand patterns  
- Underutilized and overburdened transport segments  

### 7.3 Safety & Risk Indicators
- High-risk locations based on incident frequency  
- Time windows with higher accident severity  
- Environmental factors influencing traffic safety  

---

## 8. Dashboard & Visualization

Power BI dashboards were designed with a focus on clarity and usability:

### Dashboard Sections
- **Overview:** Key KPIs and summary metrics  
- **Time Analysis:** Hourly, daily, and monthly trends  
- **Location Analysis:** Congestion and risk hotspots  
- **Mobility Insights:** Transport mode and flow patterns  

### KPIs Displayed
- Total traffic volume  
- Average speed  
- Peak-hour congestion percentage  
- High-risk location count  

---

## 9. Key Insights

- Traffic congestion is heavily concentrated during **morning and evening peak hours**  
- A small number of locations contribute to a **disproportionate share of congestion**  
- Traffic flow efficiency drops significantly when volume exceeds critical thresholds  
- Certain time periods show **higher safety risks despite lower traffic volume**  

---

## 10. Recommendations

Based on the analysis, the following recommendations are proposed:

- Optimize traffic signal timing during peak hours  
- Improve road infrastructure at high-congestion locations  
- Encourage public and non-motorized transport for short-distance travel  
- Increase monitoring and enforcement during high-risk time windows  
- Use data dashboards for continuous traffic performance tracking  

---

## 11. Limitations

- Data availability may vary across locations  
- Lack of real-time traffic data limits predictive insights  
- Environmental and behavioral factors may not be fully captured  

---

## 12. Conclusion

This project demonstrates how **end-to-end data analysis** using Python, Excel, and Power BI can uncover meaningful insights into urban mobility and traffic flow. By focusing on interpretable metrics and actionable insights, the analysis supports smarter transportation planning and more efficient urban mobility systems.
